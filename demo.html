Thrifting is cool.

Whether you're fashion-forward, on a tight budget, environmentally conscious, or opposed to human trafficking, buying clothes and other goods secondhand will benefit you, our fellow humans, and our environment.

1. Buying secondhand benefits the environment

The textile industry produces toxic waste, which creates extreme air and water pollution.

Cotton farming accounts for 25% of insecticide usage worldwide. Cotton farmers use many of the most harmful pesticides on the market, which endanger the farmworkers, wildlife, water supplies, and the environment.
